DISC Score

The Decision Integration for Strong Communities (DISC) index is a summary measure that provides a profile about select community characteristics. The index is based on indicators and metrics derived from existing cultural, economic, health, and other related data from years 2015-2019. The DISC profile index provides a snapshot about quality of life characteristics common across all communities and is sensitive to changes in the economic, social and ecological environments.

A synthesis of this knowledge helps shape our understanding about people and their needs, both actual and perceived.

Services Scores



The wants and needs of people are met through the availability of a variety of social, economic and environmental items (i.e., goods) and their delivery (i.e., services). The availability and flow of these services address many aspects of a community--everything from health to employment to clean water. New investment in or redistribution of economic, social and natural resources help to introduce changes that influence the quality of life for individuals and their communities.

Cities produce "urban heat islands" as a result of thermal pollution from heat-generating buildings, vehicles, factories, and people. Urban areas are susceptible to the "heat island" effect due to buildings and dark paved surfaces that absorb heat during the daytime and slowly release this heat at night. This increases the annual mean air temperature of a city.
