Decision Integration for Strong Communities

Communities sit at the intersection where the natural, built and social environments meet. From creating jobs to building greener infrastructure, many considerations are needed to yield the most sustainable decision outcomes for a community. Understanding what aspects about your community you value and wish to sustain or improve may help inform community planning decisions.

Indicators and indices can be used to assess various attributes about your community. Indicators summarize raw measures (e.g., median income, disease prevalence) to help interpret data patterns and trends. For communities, no single index or indicator set can offer a complete picture of status or conditions. However, an integrative approach that synthesizes information from many different indicator sets can offer broader insight from multiple viewpoints.

The Decision Integration for Strong Communities (DISC) application is a simple and intuitive tool designed to bring together information from many indicator products to help characterize quality of life in your community and present resources that may present ways to help improve aspects about your community that you value.

The Decision Integration for Strong Communities (DISC) index is a summary measure that provides a profile about select community characteristics. The index is based on indicators and metrics derived from existing cultural, economic, health, and other related data. The DISC profile index provides a snapshot about quality of life characteristics common across all communities and is sensitive to changes in the economic, social and ecological environments.
